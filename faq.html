FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions
In a sentence, what does BEARS do?

- BEARS empowers upperclassmen to help freshman take advantage of the abundant resources at Cornell University.

I'm kinda busy and I don't want to take on any more commitments?

- The ONLY requirement BEARS has is that pairs meet once a month. That’s all. And this meeting can be as simple as a 15-minute talk over coffee, which could be a study break. Also, it’s always a good idea to give back to the community.

My organization already matches me with a mentor/mentee. Is there a point in my joining BEARS?

- Yes! BEARS allows you to request a mentor or mentee. Therefore, an organization can keep it’s preexisting mentorship pairs, and simply take advantage of the resources BEARS provides! At no extra effort. Alternatively, you can find a mentor or mentee in another area of life. You can never be doing too much good.

Why should I be a mentor?

- Personal satisfaction from fostering the professional development of a student.
- Opportunities to strengthen knowledge base and improve communication skills as mentees
- Expose mentor to new ideas and perspectives.
- Improvement of coaching, leadership, teaching and counseling skills.
- Implicitly held responsible for life decisions.
- Personal growth.
- Demonstrates commitment to personal and professional development.

Why do I need mentorship?

- Receive guidance and support in the areas most important to you.
- Support during initial professional development.
- Increased confidence in the desired environment.
- Challenges to set goals and achieve personal standards.
- Provides a forum to dialogue with and receive advice from experienced professionals.
- Development of new, professional contacts.
- Development of a relationship with a person who can be a role model and sounding board for feedback on career paths.

Why BEARS?

- Empowerment. Drawing on professional literature, BEARS helps mentorship pairs get more out of the relationship by providing them with the resources they need.
- Matching. Drawing on psychological studies, pairs are formed on what participants value most, and mix in their interests to find the perfect match.
- Events. BEARS puts on monthly events to facilitate mentorships and allow pairs to meet other pairs.
- Family. BEARS provides an extended network of people with similar interests and values.
- Community. As one of the largest organizations at Cornell, you undoubtedly feel a sense of belonging to something bigger.
- Executive Board. Only the best and most talented minds serve on the board. Therefore, BEARS always maintains only highest quality of service to its members.
- Brand. The name, gear and logo make membership to BEARS something to flaunt.
